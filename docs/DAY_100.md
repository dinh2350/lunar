## Top 10 Lessons Learned

### 1. Build > Tutorial
Reading about RAG is nothing like debugging why your retrieval accuracy is 40% at 2am. Building forces you to face every edge case.

### 2. Eval is the Most Important Thing
Without automated quality measurement, you're flying blind. Eval-driven development is the AI equivalent of TDD â€” write the test first, then improve the prompt.

### 3. Hybrid Search Beats Pure Approaches
Neither keyword search nor vector search alone is sufficient. Combining BM25 + vectors with rank fusion gives dramatically better results than either alone.

### 4. Guard Rails from Day 1
Prompt injection, PII leakage, infinite loops â€” safety isn't a nice-to-have. Build the guard pipeline before anything else.

### 5. Streaming Changes Everything
Users don't care about total response time â€” they care about *perceived* response time. Streaming the first token in <500ms makes a 5-second response feel instant.

### 6. One Commit Per Day Works
Forcing yourself to ship something every day, even small, creates compound momentum. 100 small commits > 10 big ones.

### 7. TypeScript is Great for AI
Async I/O, strong typing, great ecosystem. You don't need Python to build AI systems. Use what you know.

### 8. Local LLMs are Good Enough
Ollama with 7-13B parameter models handles 90% of conversational AI use cases. You don't always need GPT-4.

### 9. The Boring Stuff Matters
Caching, error handling, backup scripts, security headers. The difference between a demo and a product is the boring stuff.

### 10. Document as You Go
Writing documentation, blog posts, and presentation materials while building â€” not after â€” makes the content 10x better because the context is fresh.
